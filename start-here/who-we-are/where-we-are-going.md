# Where We're Going

**Navigation:** [Start Here](../../index.md) → [Who We Are](./index.md) → **Where We're Going**

You joined a company with big ambitions. Here's where we're headed and how your work connects to that future.

---

## The Big Picture: Building K-Fashion's LVMH

**Our ultimate vision:** A 500 trillion won global luxury group—Korea's answer to LVMH.

LVMH owns Louis Vuitton, Dior, Givenchy, and dozens of other luxury brands. They've mastered the art of building brand portfolios at scale. We're building the same thing for K-fashion and K-entertainment convergence.

**Why this is possible:**
- K-pop has already proven Korean cultural products can dominate globally
- Fashion is the next frontier (HYBE proved the model for entertainment)
- We have unique advantages others can't replicate (see below)

---

## The Medium-Term Vision: Fashion's HYBE

**Target:** 50 trillion won valuation within 10 years.

HYBE changed the global music industry by combining K-pop talent with systematic operations and tech-driven fan engagement. We're doing the same for fashion:

- **HYBE's formula:** K-pop artists + global distribution + data-driven operations
- **Kyndof's formula:** K-fashion design + K-entertainment networks + AI-powered systems

HYBE started with BTS. We're starting with 2000Atelier's artist network and 2000Archives' retail brand.

---

## The Reality: We're a Startup

The vision is massive. The reality right now is startup life.

**What this means for you:**
- **High uncertainty** - Not everything will work. That's normal.
- **High learning** - You'll grow faster here than at an established company.
- **High stakes** - Early team members get the biggest opportunities if we succeed.

This is the tradeoff: You're betting on potential instead of stability. The vision is real, but it takes time and execution.

---

## How We Get There: The Core Hypothesis

**The bet:** Entertainment marketing creates retail supergrowth.

Here's the strategy:
1. **2000Atelier** builds relationships with K-pop artists through custom stage costumes
2. Those artists become authentic brand ambassadors for **2000Archives** retail
3. Their fans become our customers (proven effect: artist endorsement dramatically increases sales)
4. This creates marketing power that traditional fashion brands can't match

**Why this works:**
- We're not paying for influencer marketing—we're building organic relationships through craft
- Artists wear our designs on stage, creating massive exposure
- The connection is authentic (they trust our work because we make their stage outfits)

**The moat:** You can't replicate this overnight. Building artist relationships takes years of quality delivery.

## Why We'll Win: Intersection Strategy

**We sit at the intersection of three powerful trends:**

1. **K-fashion** - Korean design sensibility and manufacturing
2. **K-pop** - Global entertainment dominance and fan culture
3. **AI tech** - Automation that lets us scale without traditional overhead

**Why this is a moat:**
- Very few companies have expertise in all three areas
- You can't build these capabilities quickly (relationships take years)
- The synergies compound over time (each reinforces the others)

Most fashion companies have #1. Some have #2. Almost none have #3. We're building all three.

---

## Measuring Progress: The OKR System

**We use OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) to track whether we're on track.**

- **Objective** - What we want to achieve (qualitative)
- **Key Results** - How we measure success (quantitative)

**Example:**
- **Objective:** Establish 2000Archives as a leading K-fashion brand
- **Key Result 1:** Achieve 1 billion won in quarterly revenue
- **Key Result 2:** 20%+ repeat customer rate
- **Key Result 3:** 3+ major artist collaborations per quarter

Your team's OKRs are documented in Notion. Check them to see how you're contributing.

---

## Systems Over Heroes

**Traditional fashion companies rely on "the genius designer" or "the star merchandiser."**

**We believe:** When that person leaves, the company collapses.

**Our approach:** Build systems that outlast individuals.

- When you solve a problem, document how you solved it
- When you learn something, add it to the wiki
- When you build a process, write the SOP

**Why this matters:** Your knowledge becomes permanent value for the company (and you get credit for that).
